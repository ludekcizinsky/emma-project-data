ID: mc2zl2t # OF UPVOTES: 2.0 SUBREDDIT: ethicalhacking
COMMENT TEXT: You can work with any distro to do hacking, it's more about learning the system you're working with and understanding your target system's ins and outs. Then you need to learn and understand the tools you're working with. Kali has become a standard because it comes pre-loaded with so many tools. The others require hunting down the tools you want to use, downloading them, and then working with them. Ubuntu is a great system, and many professionals use it or Arch (Black arch is also an option if you want something pre-loaded with tools.) 

That said, you can do ethical hacking from windows, or even a mac if you're feeling froggy. It just depends on the sort of interface and experience you're looking for
